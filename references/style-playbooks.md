# Style Playbooks

Choose one playbook, then adapt its tokens to the product instead of copying it verbatim. Do not mix directions within the same screen.

## Quiet Studio

Use for writing, research, productivity, and focused creative tools.

- Canvas: cool off-white or pale gray; surfaces: clean white; accent: saturated cobalt or emerald.
- Type: a calm sans-serif with a compact heading scale and generous reading line-height.
- Layout: persistent left navigation, a centered work column, and light separators instead of heavy card borders.
- Details: show progress, metadata, and secondary actions quietly. Let the working content dominate.

## Signal Console

Use for monitoring, automation, data, and operations workflows.

- Canvas: near-black charcoal; surfaces: graphite; accents: electric lime, cyan, and semantic status colors.
- Type: sturdy sans-serif for controls and tabular numerals for metrics.
- Layout: dense but ordered panels, explicit labels, fixed-height toolbars, and clear status grouping.
- Details: use color for meaning, not decoration. Pair every signal color with a label, icon, or value.

## Editorial Ledger

Use for knowledge bases, reports, catalogs, and comparison-heavy products.

- Canvas: neutral light gray; surfaces: white; accents: deep blue and restrained coral.
- Type: a distinctive display face only for page-level titles, with a highly legible sans-serif for working text.
- Layout: deliberate columns, strong baseline alignment, visible section rhythm, and tables optimized for scanning.
- Details: surface provenance, timestamps, and filters as structured metadata rather than visual noise.

## Warm Workshop

Use for builders, collaborative tools, and approachable creation workflows.

- Canvas: soft blue-gray; surfaces: white; accents: indigo, leaf green, and apricot for emphasis.
- Type: open sans-serif proportions with friendly but not oversized headings.
- Layout: visible next actions, compact tool clusters, and direct manipulation where the product supports it.
- Details: use subtle motion to confirm actions and unobtrusive helper copy near complex controls.

## Applying A Playbook

1. Map the playbook colors to semantic tokens before styling components.
2. Apply the system to the page shell and core interaction first.
3. Add only details that clarify state, ownership, urgency, or hierarchy.
4. Review each repeated component against the chosen direction and remove exceptions that do not serve the task.
